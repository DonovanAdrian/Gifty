Creating An Account

    While on the login page, also known as "index", click/tap on the "Don't have an account? Click here!" message. The resulting page will ask you for required user profile information.

User Profile Fields:

Full Name: This field will be where you enter your full name (First and Last), so that way your friends and family can easily identify who you are.

User Name: This field will be what you share with your friends and family to invite them to see what you have on your gift list. It can be as plain, creative, or silly as you'd like. Make sure that it is something unique, because if it's taken, then you'll have to pick something else!

Pin: This is your desired pin/number combination to log into Gifty. Keep this a secret and use a pin you have never used before. No computer system is 100% secure, so your due diligence as a user will be critical in protecting your pins.

Confirm Pin: This is where you will confirm your pin to verify that you entered your pin correctly!

    Once you have completed providing your desired user profile information, click/tap "Create User Profile" to create your account. Done! You will be redirected back to the login page to use your new account!

The Lists Page -> The Public List Page

    The Public Gift List is fairly self explanatory! It's the public list of gifts that your friend would like. The gifts that appear in this list are what your friend added on their home page. Not to worry, though! They can't see whether or not any of these gifts are bought. Please note that you can also quickly swap between the Public List and the Private list by clicking on the button on the bottom left of the page!

    Once you've bought a gift, you may notice that it is crossed off in either red or blue! A red gift means that it's been bought and that your friend only wanted one of that specific gift. A blue gift, however, means that it's been bought and that your friend is okay with receiving multiples of that specific gift. For example, if they want a bunch of pairs of socks or lots of gift cards, they could ask for multiples!

    But what if someone else buys a gift at the exact same time you do? Not to worry! As long as you have a strong internet connection, the gift list updates in real time! You can rest easy knowing that your bought gifts are communicated to your friend's friends as well, as long as they are keeping a close watch on the gift list, too.

    If your friend happens to update or remove their gift from their public gift list after you've bought it, not to worry! You will be sent a notification to ensure that you're up to date on whether or not you need to return the gift you already bought.

The Lists Page -> The Private List Page

    You may now know what the Public List is, but what is the Private List? The Private List is a private gift list which your friend cannot see. The gifts that appear in this list are what your friend's friends added on the home page, so this is a list where your friend's friends can all collaborate and surprise your common buddy! For example, if someone overhears that your common friend wants something in particular, but they don't add it to their public list, someone can add it to their private list for a sweet surprise! Please note that you can also quickly swap between the Public List and the Private list by clicking on the button on the bottom left of the page!

    From here, you can add a gift on the bottom right of the page. Once you've added a gift, your friend's friends can all see the gift appear and any changes that happen to it, too. Please note that only you, the gift creator, can remove the private gifts that you create! However, any of the common friends can update this gift in your stead just in case corrections are needed.

    Once you've bought a gift, you may notice that it is crossed off in either red or blue! A red gift means that it's been bought and that someone indicated that your common friend only wanted one of that specific gift. A blue gift, however, means that it's been bought and that someone indicated that your common friend is okay with receiving multiples of that specific gift.

    But what if someone else buys a gift at the exact same time you do? Not to worry! As long as you have a strong internet connection, the gift list updates in real time! You can rest easy knowing that your bought gifts are communicated to your friend's friends as well, as long as they are keeping a close watch on the gift list, too.

    If someone happens to update the private gift from the gift list after you've bought it, not to worry! You will be sent a notification to ensure that you're up to date on whether or not you need to return the gift you already bought.

The Friends Page

    The Friends Page is where you can invite new friends to see your Gifty profile and where any existing friends are stored. When someone else accepts an invite to your friend list or if you accept a friend invite from someone, you will both be able to see each other's gift lists.

Sending Invites
    Are your Lists Page or Friends Page looking a little scarce or you just need to invite some new friends to add to your friend list? This is the place! Click/Tap on the "Invite User" button on the bottom right of the page to send a friend request. However, be sure to fetch either your friend's User Name or Share Code from them in order to send an invite, otherwise this won't work. Once you've sent the invite, it will be sent to your friend's pending invites.

    If you have any friends that you might have in common with someone, but you haven't sent them an invite, your "Send Invite" button may appear green. If you click on the "Send Invite" button in this case, you will see a suggested friend that you can send an invite to. If you don't want this friend to be suggested to you in the future, however, then you can click/tap on the "X" next to their name to remove them from the suggestions pool.

The Notifications Page

    The Notification Page can be found on almost any of the Gifty webpages in the form of a bell icon! Please note that there are some pages where the notification icon will not appear, such as The Settings Page. If the bell is black and white, then you do not have any pending notifications! If the bell icon is slowly flashing red, then you have some pending notifications. Check them out! Simply click/tap on the bell icon at the bottom middle of the page. After clicking/tapping on the bell icon, the resulting page will take you to the list of pending or read notifications that you may have. This is where any important messages for you are located! Some examples of possible notifications are private messages, friend requests, and notifications letting you know that a gift you bought has been deleted/updated.

    A notification will automatically be marked as "read" if you open it. Click/tap on a given notification to view the message inside! A pop-up will open and display the notification's title and message. If you'd like to delete a specific notification, click/tap "Delete Notification" on the bottom right of the pop-up window. If you'd like to remove all of your notifications, you can click/tap the "Remove All Notifications" button on the bottom right of the Notifications Page.

Moderating Gifty

Moderation Considerations
    Any user that has the "moderation" role has a lot of power in Gifty. It is important to be careful who you grant access to this role, as they could cause unnecessary damage to the Gifty users and their experience. At the end of the day, however, the user that set up the Gifty server and/or users who have access to the database have absolute control over the Gifty experience.

Configurable Variables
    Although this is exclusive to the individual who set up the Gifty server, there are variables that can be easily accessed which can affect the Gifty user's experience. These variables can be found near the top of the "common.js" file. While there are other variables that are littered across Gifty that can also affect a Gifty user's experience, only those on the "common.js" file should be changed.

The Moderation Page

Moderation

(Example Moderation Button)


    Once on the Settings Page, click/tap on the "Moderation" button on the bottom right of the page. Please note that you can only see this button if you are a moderator. The moderation page is where you can quickly view a summary of all of your users - and their activity - across Gifty. In addition to this, you can also keep an eye on if any audit log tickets are received. In effect, this is the "main hub" for moderators. There are also various configurable settings that can be updated to enhance your moderation experience. Continue reading for more details.

Viewing and Moderating User Accounts
    By clicking/tapping on a user, you can view a quick summary of all of their details. You can also manually sign them up for Secret Santa, view their gift lists, view their notifications, and other in-depth information all in one place (Please note that this view may differ depending on your personal moderation settings. This can be changed in the Moderation Settings section specified below).

Warning and Banning Users
    If you notice that some of your users' actions are getting out of hand, then you can send them warnings or ban them as needed. You will receive an audit log notification when a given user has opened their warning, which will be sent to their notifications page. When you ban a user, the login page will simply appear as if they have forgotten their password. You will additionally receive an audit log notification when a banned user tries to log in.

    To warn a user, simply click/tap on the user you'd like to warn, then click/tap on "Warn" on the bottom left of the popup window. A popup window will appear in which you can type the warning you'd like to notify the user for. Once you have provided your message, click/tap on "Send" on the bottom left of the message window. You have now successfully warned a user.

Moderation Settings
    To view the Moderation Settings, click/tap on the gear icon on the bottom left of the Moderation Page. A popup window will then appear where you can change various database and general settings which will affect the Gifty experience in one way or another.

Database Options

   Remove All User's Notifications:
      This button removes all Gifty user's notifications and is an irreversible action.

   Reset All User's Scores:
      This button resets all Gifty user's scores to zero and is an irreversible action.

   Enable/Disable Gift URL Limiter:
      This limiter is meant to restrict what kinds of URLs your users can link to in their gifts. The Gift URL Limiter is disabled by default, but when activated, the default is "amazon,amzn,bestbuy,barnesandnoble". For example, www.amazon.com would be valid with this default.

   Set Database Limits:
      This limiter is meant to restrict the amount of users and gifts that your Gifty server will accept. This cannot be disabled, but you can set each limit to a large number to "disable" it. The Database limits are defaulted to 100 users and 50 gifts per each user's list.


General Options

   Hide Extra Data Points:
      This setting is meant to de-clutter your user interface on the Moderation Page. This setting is per-Moderator and is defaulted to "hidden". When extra data points are "hidden", some data points (like Secret Santa) will appear and disappear if they apply per-user.

   Show Quick Info:
      This setting is meant to give you a quick view of specific user information in the user list. This setting is per-Moderator and is defaulted to "None".

   Send Global Message:
      This button allows you to send Global Notifications to all Gifty users, including yourself. This notification will be sent to each user's Notification Page.

   Disable Login Function:
      This button allows you to disable login functionality on Gifty if your Gifty is experiencing significant issues. This will block all normal users from logging in. Please note that moderators are still able to login while this is active.

   Disable Gift Limiter:
      This button blocks users who are related to each other from viewing their lists. Please note that "related" in this case means that a moderator set a specific user to be a parent/child of another user. This is meant to dissuade a parent from viewing who has bought their gifts by logging in as their child.


User Creation Override
    If you have a user limit is set on your Gifty database, but you'd like to create an extra account for someone, you can use the user creation override. While on the login page, or "index", you can input your user credentials and click the "Gifty Database Full! Existing Users Can Still Log In Above!" message. This will take you to the user creation page, where you can create a new account despite the Gifty user limit.

A final pop-up window will appear to confirm that the user you are requesting is correct. Click/tap on "Yes" to add the user to the family.

   Parent/Child Relationships:
      If you have any users that are under age who cannot use Gifty, like an infant or toddler, you can set them to be "related" to their parent in Gifty. Please note that you cannot relate users in this family to users in a separate family. By doing adding users as a parent or child to each other, they will not be assigned to each other during Secret Santa. You can also optionally enable the Gift List Limiter on the Moderation Page, which will prevent a child user from viewing a parent's list and vis versa. This is meant to prevent a parent from viewing what has been bought on their gift list through their child's account.

The Family Page -> Secret Santa Operation

Hiding or Showing The Secret Santa Controls
    Hidden controls are set on a per-family basis. If you have a family that you never need Secret Santa controls for, then their controls can remain hidden for as long as you need it to be. If automatic control is enabled and the Secret Santa controls are hidden, automatic control will still run in the background.

Secret Santa States
There are three Secret Santa States. The three Secret Santa states are as follows:
   Idle State:
      This state is when Secret Santa is idle. Nothing is happening and each family member will not see any Secret Santa prompts. This state will also reset any Secret Santa settings for every family member!
   Ready State:
      This state is when Secret Santa is ready to assign users. Each family member will see a "Sign Up" button on the "Gift Lists" page. Once enough users are signed up (4), then the next state can be activated.
   Active State:
      This state is when Secret Santa is fully active. Each family member has been assigned a user to buy a gift for. Let the gifting begin!

Secret Santa Statuses
    If there is a failure upon manually changing states, it will show up here. More failure details will appear in the audit log. If automatic control is running, the upcoming state change will be specified here.

Testing Assignments
    Testing Assignments allows you to test a given family's assignments without actually performing any permanent assignments. The purpose of this button is to verify whether or not your family has enough family members and has enough family members signed up for the Secret Santa activity.

Automatic Control
    The key to automatic control is user activity. Automatic state changes will occur if a user in a given family navigates to the Lists Page. Please note that moderator users will trigger automatic state changes for all families if they are on the Lists Page or the Family Page. If users are not active in a given family, then Automatic Control may be automatically disabled. Automatic control can be disabled through skipped states, manual state changes while automatic control is active, and automatic state change errors. Audit Log tickets are delivered for each of these cases. If automatic control is automatically disabled, then manual control must be performed to go to the next state for a given family. If you manually change the state while automatic control is enabled, automatic control will be disabled. If there are any errors changing states or assigning users, automatic control will be disabled. There is a limit to how many families can have automatic control enabled at one time. This amount is configurable if absolutely necessary in the "common.js" file (automaticSecretSantaLimit), but the individual who set up the Gifty server will need to adjust it. It is not recommended to update this variable, as it could have potentially adverse affects on the Gifty user's experience if misused.

Exporting Secret Santa Data
    Exporting Secret Santa Data is a great way to quickly get a summary of a given family's Secret Santa data in a document format. The type of document that gets generated will depend on what state the given family is in and will export all of the relevant data pertaining to that state and the users in that family.

Considerations and Caveats
    Secret Santa is a great way to enhance the experience of your families when using Gifty, but there are some things to consider before you jump in! As you can see above, there are plenty of details that go into this process. Once you know how to operate Secret Santa from the moderator's perspective, however, then your family or families can all have tons of fun for the holidays.

   Minimum Amount Of Sign Ups:
      One thing that you must consider is that there must be a minimum amount of users that can be signed up to Secret Santa in a family before they can be assigned. For example, a family of two users doesn't make for a very fun Secret Santa experience. Please note that each member who signs up can only be assigned to another user in that family who also signed up! The more family members you have signed up, the better!

   Friends and Relationships:
      If you have any relationships applied to your users or if your family members aren't all friends with each other, then this can heavily affect your Secret Santa experience. Each user's friend list is their list of potential Secret Santa matches. As a result, the less friends they have, the less possibilities of assignments they have, too. The same idea applies for parent/child relationships, as this will further restrict how many possible assignments they could have.


Potential Secret Santa Errors
    If you are one of the lucky few moderators who have noticed that I intentionally spit out lots of in-depth information into your browser's console log, then Secret Santa is no exception. Every time you "Test Assignments" or assign users for real, a "Result Code" will be output into the console log. These Result Codes do actually correspond with what occurred while assigning users. Please note that errors, whether during assignments or automatic control, will also be sent to the Audit Log. The Result Codes are as follows:

      -1 is a processing error, there's something very wrong
      0 (Zero) is a success
      1 is a failure to find a viable "Giver" user
      2 is a user that had ZERO potential assignments
      3 is a Secret Santa recipient that was duplicated
      4 is a Secret Santa recipient that was not properly assigned
      5 Verification Step, a Secret Santa giver was duplicated
      6 Verification Step, a Secret Santa recipient was duplicated
      7 Verification Step, a Secret Santa giver was not in the same family
      8 Verification Step, a Secret Santa recipient was not in the same family
      9 Verification Step, a Secret Santa giver was NOT assigned a Secret Santa name
      10 Verification Step, Givers, Recipients, and Family Member count mismatch
      11 Verification Step, Giver and Receiver records do not match
      12 Completion Step, Error Saving records to DB
